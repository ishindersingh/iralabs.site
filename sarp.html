Founder Role & Equity Proposal

Joint Venture Partnership Agreement
IRA Labs & Techcadd

1. Executive Overview

This proposal establishes the operational framework, resource allocation, compensation structure, and equity distribution for the joint venture between IRA Labs and Techcadd. The document outlines my role as operational founder and lead researcher, the investment and support provided by Techcadd, and the rationale for the proposed 50-50 equity split that ensures sustainable growth and mutual success as true equal partners.

2. Strategic Context & Academic Considerations

To commit full-time operational leadership to this venture while maintaining academic obligations, I require formal employment documentation from Techcadd. This enables me to:

  • Obtain University NOC: Secure a No Objection Certificate from my university that permits full-time professional work alongside degree completion
  • Academic compliance: Demonstrate legitimate employment status required by institutional regulations
  • Professional credibility: Establish formal employment relationship that validates my commitment to both academic and professional responsibilities
  • Legal clarity: Create proper documentation for all stakeholders including family, university administration, and business partners

This arrangement allows me to dedicate complete operational focus to the venture while maintaining academic progress, ensuring neither commitment compromises the other.

3. Founder Responsibilities & Operational Scope

As the operational founder and lead researcher, I will assume comprehensive responsibility for building, managing, and scaling all core business functions:

3.1 Product Development & Content Creation

  • Designing comprehensive curriculum for AI, robotics, electronics, and coding programs
  • Recording, editing, and producing professional video course content
  • Developing both online and offline educational materials
  • Creating supporting documentation, tutorials, and learning resources

3.2 Operations & Team Management

  • Managing daily operational workflow and project execution
  • Recruiting and overseeing interns, trainers, video editors, and support staff
  • Establishing operational systems, processes, and quality standards
  • Coordinating cross-functional activities and ensuring deadline adherence

3.3 Marketing & Brand Development

  • Developing and executing comprehensive marketing strategies
  • Managing social media growth across YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn, and other platforms
  • Creating content calendars, campaigns, and engagement systems
  • Building brand authority and community presence in the AI & robotics education sector
  • Website management, SEO optimization, and digital marketing automation

3.4 Research, Development & Product Innovation

  • Conducting R&D for educational robotics kits and AI projects
  • Designing electronics products and learning tools
  • Managing vendor relationships, production processes, and inventory control
  • Ensuring product quality standards and continuous improvement

3.5 Community Engagement & Business Development

  • Organizing workshops, demonstrations, and educational events
  • Developing school partnership programs and institutional relationships
  • Managing customer support, pre-order systems, and retention strategies
  • Creating pitch decks, business presentations, and strategic documentation

3.6 Research Leadership & Innovation

  • Lead Researcher Role: Serving as chief research officer responsible for identifying emerging technology trends and investment opportunities
  • Technology Scouting: Researching and evaluating new technologies where the company can invest for long-term strategic advantage and profitability
  • Exhibition Development: Designing and building showcase robots, drones, and automation systems for exhibitions, trade shows, and promotional events
  • Innovation Pipeline: Creating prototype demonstrations that establish market leadership and attract institutional partnerships
  • Competitive Intelligence: Monitoring global technology developments to position company at forefront of emerging opportunities
  • Strategic R&D: Leading applied research projects that generate intellectual property and competitive differentiation

4. Techcadd Investment & Resource Commitment

Techcadd will provide the financial infrastructure and resources necessary for successful launch and sustainable growth:

4.1 Financial Investment

  • Complete funding for initial setup, equipment, and operational expenses (?7 lakh initial investment)
  • Monthly salary budget for founder and staff
  • Marketing and advertising budget allocation
  • Working capital for inventory, materials, and production costs

4.2 Infrastructure & Facilities

  • Office and laboratory space for operations, production, and content creation
  • Recording studio setup with professional video and audio equipment
  • Hardware, tools, and robotics materials for product development
  • Technology infrastructure including computers, software, and networking

4.3 Corporate Support & Network Access

  • Legal support for company registration, compliance, and contracts
  • Access to established business networks and industry connections
  • Brand leverage from existing Techcadd reputation and credibility
  • Administrative and back-office support systems

5. Founder Compensation: Monthly Salary Requirement

Proposed Monthly Salary: ?40,000

Strategic Rationale:

A fixed monthly salary is essential for ensuring sustained productivity, strategic focus, and professional execution. This compensation structure addresses fundamental economic realities that directly impact venture success:

5.1 Productivity & Focus Benefits

  • Elimination of financial stress: Removes survival-mode thinking that diverts cognitive resources from strategic work
  • Full-time commitment: Enables complete dedication to venture growth without need for side income sources
  • Consistent output: Financial stability supports regular work schedules and deadline adherence
  • Mental bandwidth: Frees cognitive capacity for creative problem-solving and strategic planning rather than financial concerns

5.2 Professional Execution Standards

  • Long-term planning capability: Financial security enables multi-month strategic initiatives rather than reactive short-term decisions
  • Professional presence: Maintains appropriate appearance, resources, and credibility in stakeholder meetings
  • Quality standards: Allows investment in professional development, tools, and resources that enhance work quality
  • Reliability: Ensures consistent availability and responsiveness without financial pressure to prioritize other income

5.3 Risk Management & Retention

  • Founder retention: Competitive compensation reduces risk of operational founder departure during critical growth phases
  • Opportunity cost coverage: Compensates for alternative employment opportunities foregone to build the venture
  • Motivation alignment: Combines immediate compensation (salary) with long-term incentives (equity) for balanced motivation structure

6. Proposed Equity Structure

Equity Distribution: 80-20 Split

OPERATIONAL FOUNDER
80%
IRA Labs

Full Execution, IP Creation, Research & Operations

INVESTMENT PARTNER
20%
Techcadd

Capital, Infrastructure & Strategic Support

6.1 Equity Allocation Rationale

The 80-20 distribution reflects the fundamental reality that this venture's success depends primarily on operational execution, intellectual property creation, and full-time founder commitment, while capital investment, though critical, represents a more limited contribution that is fully compensated through substantial equity ownership despite the smaller percentage.

Contribution Factor IRA Labs (80%) Techcadd (20%)
Financial Investment Forgone salary for 12+ months (?4.8 lakh+ opportunity cost) ?7 lakh initial capital investment
Operational Responsibility 100% of daily execution, management, and decision-making Strategic guidance and advisory support
Time Commitment Full-time (60-80 hours/week including research) Part-time oversight (2-5 hours/week)
Intellectual Property 100% of course content, curriculum, systems, research innovations, prototype designs, brand development Existing brand name and network access
Risk Profile Career opportunity cost, full-time commitment, foregone income, personal reputation Financial capital risk (recoverable through operations)
Innovation & Research 100% of technology scouting, R&D leadership, prototype development, IP generation Funding provision for research initiatives
Revenue Generation Direct responsibility for all customer acquisition, content creation, teaching, and sales Indirect support through infrastructure
Replaceability Irreplaceable: venture cannot succeed without founder's specific skills, vision, and execution Replaceable: capital can be sourced from alternative investors if needed

6.2 Why 80-20 Is Fair and Standard

This equity structure aligns with market norms for ventures where one partner provides full-time operational execution and intellectual property creation while another provides primarily capital investment.

Industry Standard Validation

  • Startup Equity Norms: Typical seed-stage investments of ?5-10 lakh receive 10-25% equity when investing in established founders with proven execution capability
  • Sweat Equity Recognition: Full-time founder commitment for 12 months represents ?4.8+ lakh in foregone salary (?40,000 × 12), plus the immense value of expertise and execution
  • IP Ownership: All course content, curriculum, systems, and innovations are created entirely by the operational founder, representing the primary business asset
  • Risk Distribution: Techcadd's capital investment risk is significantly lower than founder's career risk; capital can be recovered, but time and career opportunity cannot

Fair Compensation for Each Party

For IRA Labs (80% Equity Holder):

  • Reflects true contribution: 80% equity acknowledges that the founder creates 100% of the intellectual property, executes 100% of daily operations, generates 100% of revenue through direct teaching and sales, and leads 100% of research and innovation
  • Compensates opportunity cost: Founder foregoes alternative employment income of ?4.8+ lakh annually, plus career advancement opportunities in established companies
  • Rewards irreplaceable value: The specific vision, technical skills, teaching ability, and execution capacity of the founder cannot be hired or replaced; this is what makes the venture viable
  • Accounts for risk asymmetry: Founder risks 2-3 years of career development during critical early professional years; this career opportunity cost far exceeds the financial capital at risk

For Techcadd (20% Equity Holder):

  • Excellent ROI potential: 20% of a successful venture worth ?1-2 crore in 3-5 years yields ?20-40 lakh value from ?7 lakh investment—a 3-6x return without operational involvement
  • Passive investment nature: Techcadd provides capital and infrastructure but does not perform daily work, create intellectual property, or generate direct revenue; 20% fairly compensates this support role
  • Lower time commitment: 2-5 hours/week advisory time versus founder's 60-80 hours/week full-time execution; compensation reflects this 1:15 time ratio
  • Capital recoverability: If venture fails, infrastructure assets (equipment, furniture) can be recovered and redeployed; founder's invested time and foregone career opportunities cannot
  • Continued business relationship: 20% equity provides meaningful upside participation while preserving positive long-term business relationship and partnership goodwill

Comparison to Alternative Scenarios

If Techcadd hired an equivalent executive team:

  • CEO/Operations Director: ?60,000-80,000/month
  • Content Creator/Curriculum Designer: ?40,000-50,000/month
  • Marketing Manager: ?35,000-45,000/month
  • Lead Researcher: ?50,000-70,000/month
  • Total monthly cost: ?1,85,000-2,45,000 (?22-29 lakh annually)
  • Plus: No equity ownership of IP, no founder-level commitment, high turnover risk

Conclusion: Providing 80% equity to a founder who consolidates all these roles at ?40,000/month salary represents exceptional value for Techcadd while fairly compensating the founder's multi-role contribution.

7. Strategic Benefits of Proposed Structure

7.1 For Techcadd

  • Exceptional return potential: 20% equity stake in venture projected to reach ?1-2 crore valuation yields ?20-40 lakh return on ?7 lakh investment—3-6x ROI without operational involvement
  • Zero operational burden: Fully autonomous founder handles 100% of daily operations, eliminating need for Techcadd management time or oversight
  • Innovation leadership: Access to cutting-edge research, prototypes, and showcase projects that enhance Techcadd's overall market positioning
  • Risk mitigation: Founder's ?40,000 monthly salary is covered by operations within 2-3 months; initial investment recoverable through early revenue
  • Passive investment model: 2-5 hours/week advisory time versus active management requirements of owned operations
  • Long-term relationship value: Fair equity split preserves positive partnership and opens doors to future collaborations

7.2 For the Venture

  • Maximum founder motivation: 80% ownership ensures founder captures majority of value created through their work, driving exceptional commitment and performance
  • Decision-making efficiency: Clear majority holder (80%) enables rapid strategic decisions without deadlock risk
  • Talent retention: Substantial equity ownership eliminates risk of founder departure to other opportunities
  • Quality execution: ?40,000 monthly salary removes financial stress while 80% equity provides long-term wealth creation incentive
  • IP ownership alignment: Founder who creates all content and innovations also owns majority equity, ensuring intellectual property remains with the company
  • Investor confidence: Future investors view founder-majority ownership as positive signal of commitment and alignment

8. Commitment & Vision Statement

Under this proposed 80-20 equity structure, I commit to:

  • Full-time dedication: Complete professional focus on building and scaling the venture (60-80 hours/week)
  • Operational excellence: Maintaining highest standards across product development, operations, marketing, and customer engagement
  • Research leadership: Continuously identifying emerging technologies and investment opportunities that position the company for long-term growth and profitability
  • Innovation showcase: Developing cutting-edge robots, drones, and automation systems for exhibitions and events that establish market leadership
  • Long-term commitment: Building the venture as a multi-year commitment (minimum 5-year vision), not a short-term project
  • Professional discipline: Consistent execution, transparent communication, and systematic progress tracking
  • Strategic ambition: Positioning the brand among India's leading technology education and robotics companies
  • Financial responsibility: Ensuring Techcadd's investment generates strong returns through profitable operations and sustainable growth

Shared Vision: This 80-20 equity structure creates optimal conditions for building a globally competitive AI and robotics education brand. The majority founder ownership ensures decisive leadership and maximum execution speed, while Techcadd's strategic 20% stake provides meaningful financial upside and continued partnership value. Together, we will establish a company that not only educates but also pioneers new technologies and market opportunities, delivering exceptional returns to both partners.
